The Problem

Teachers don't have time.
AI promises to fix that.
But most tools don't.

Let's be honest — the AI hype is real, but most tools waste time rather than save it. You've probably tried ChatGPT and found yourself spending 20 minutes getting something you could've written in 5. That's not a you problem. It's a workflow problem. Today I'll show you what actually sticks. Pause here — ask: "Who has already used AI for lesson prep?" (get a feel for the room)
The 5 Things

What actually saves time:

  • 1. Generating first drafts of worksheets
  • 2. Turning long texts into student summaries
  • 3. Writing differentiated versions of tasks
  • 4. Creating quiz questions from any material
  • 5. Explaining concepts in multiple ways
Walk through each point briefly — don't go deep yet, just name them. The goal here is to give people a mental map before we dive into demos. Key message: none of these replace the teacher's judgment.
